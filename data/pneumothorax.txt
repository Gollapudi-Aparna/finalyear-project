Collapsed Lung (Pneumothorax)

Overview A collapsed lung occurs when air escapes from the lung and
fills the space between the lung and the chest wall. This buildup of air
puts pressure on the lung and prevents it from expanding normally during
breathing. The medical term for this condition is pneumothorax.

Causes 1. Injury - Gunshot wound to the chest - Knife wound - Rib
fracture - Certain medical procedures

2.  Air Blisters (Blebs) Small air blisters on the lung surface may
    burst and leak air into the surrounding space. This can occur during
    scuba diving or high-altitude travel.

3.  Risk Factors

-   Tall and thin body type
-   Smoking
-   Previous pneumothorax

4.  Lung Diseases

-   Asthma
-   Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)
-   Cystic fibrosis
-   Tuberculosis
-   Whooping cough

5.  Spontaneous Pneumothorax Sometimes a collapsed lung occurs without
    any clear cause.

Symptoms

Common Symptoms - Sharp chest or shoulder pain (worse with deep
breathing or coughing) - Shortness of breath - Nasal flaring

Severe Symptoms (Large Pneumothorax) - Bluish skin color (cyanosis) -
Chest tightness - Lightheadedness or fainting - Easy fatigue - Rapid
heart rate - Shock or collapse

Diagnosis Doctors may detect decreased or absent breath sounds on the
affected side.

Tests may include: - Chest X-ray - Arterial blood gas test - CT scan -
Electrocardiogram (ECG)

Treatment

Small Pneumothorax - May resolve on its own - Oxygen therapy - Rest

Needle Aspiration A needle may be used to remove air from around the
lung to allow re-expansion.

Chest Tube For large pneumothorax: - Tube inserted between ribs - Air
drained from chest cavity - Hospital stay may be required

Surgery - Repair of leaking area - Pleurodesis (chemical treatment to
prevent recurrence)

Prognosis Risk of recurrence increases if: - Person is tall and thin -
Person continues smoking - Previous episodes occurred

Outcome depends on the underlying cause.

Complications - Recurrence of collapsed lung - Shock - Infection -
Severe lung inflammation

Prevention - No guaranteed prevention - Avoid smoking - Follow proper
scuba diving safety procedures

Alternative Names - Air around the lung - Air outside the lung - Dropped
lung - Spontaneous pneumothorax
