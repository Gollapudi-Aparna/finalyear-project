Edema

Overview Edema is swelling caused by too much fluid trapped in the
body’s tissues. It can affect any part of the body but most commonly
appears in the legs and feet.

Edema may be caused by medicines, pregnancy, or underlying diseases such
as: - Congestive heart failure - Kidney disease - Venous insufficiency -
Cirrhosis of the liver

Reducing salt intake and wearing compression garments may help relieve
symptoms. If edema is caused by a disease, the underlying disease must
be treated.

Symptoms - Swelling or puffiness under the skin (especially legs or
arms) - Stretched or shiny skin - Skin that holds a dimple after being
pressed (pitting edema) - Swelling of the abdomen (ascites) - Feeling of
leg heaviness

Emergency Symptoms (Seek immediate care) - Shortness of breath -
Irregular heartbeat - Chest pain

These may indicate pulmonary edema (fluid buildup in the lungs), which
is life-threatening.

Other Warning Signs After sitting for long periods (such as long
flights), one-sided leg swelling and calf pain may indicate deep vein
thrombosis (DVT), which requires immediate medical attention.

Causes

Edema occurs when small blood vessels (capillaries) leak fluid into
surrounding tissues.

Common Causes of Mild Edema - Sitting or staying in one position too
long - Eating too much salt - Premenstrual changes - Pregnancy

Medication-Related Causes - High blood pressure medicines - Nonsteroidal
anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) - Steroids - Estrogens - Certain
diabetes medicines (thiazolidinediones) - Medicines used to treat nerve
pain

Serious Medical Conditions That Cause Edema

Congestive Heart Failure Occurs when the heart does not pump blood
effectively. Blood backs up in the legs and abdomen. Fluid may also
accumulate in the lungs (pulmonary edema).

Liver Disease (Cirrhosis) Causes fluid buildup in the abdomen (ascites)
and legs.

Kidney Disease Causes fluid and salt buildup, leading to swelling in
legs and around the eyes.

Nephrotic Syndrome Low blood protein levels due to kidney damage can
result in edema.

Chronic Venous Insufficiency Damage to leg vein valves causes blood
pooling and leg swelling.

Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) Blood clot in a leg vein causes sudden
swelling and calf pain.

Lymphatic System Damage Cancer surgery or other conditions may damage
lymph drainage, leading to swelling.

Severe Protein Deficiency Long-term lack of protein in diet can lead to
edema.

Risk Factors - Pregnancy - Certain medications - Chronic illnesses
(heart, liver, kidney disease) - Surgery involving lymph nodes

Complications (If Untreated) - Increasing pain and swelling - Difficulty
walking - Joint stiffness - Skin itching and stretching - Increased
infection risk - Scarring between tissue layers - Reduced blood flow -
Higher risk of skin ulcers

When to See a Doctor Make an appointment for persistent swelling, shiny
or stretched skin, or pitting edema. Seek immediate care for shortness
of breath, chest pain, or irregular heartbeat.
