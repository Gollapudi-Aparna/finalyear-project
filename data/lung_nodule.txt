Disease Name: Lung Nodule

Overview:
A lung nodule is a small round or oval growth in the lung. It is usually found during chest X-rays or CT scans. Lung nodules are common and many are noncancerous. A nodule is defined as a spot in the lung that is smaller than 3 centimeters in diameter.

Symptoms:
Most lung nodules do not cause symptoms. When symptoms occur they may include:
Persistent cough
Shortness of breath
Chest discomfort
Coughing up blood in rare cases

Causes:
Old infections
Tuberculosis
Fungal infections
Inflammation
Scar tissue
Benign tumors
Early lung cancer

Risk Factors:
Smoking
Exposure to secondhand smoke
Air pollution
Exposure to harmful chemicals
Family history of lung disease

Diagnosis:
Chest X-ray
CT scan
PET scan
Biopsy
Sputum tests

Treatment:
Monitoring with regular scans
Medication if infection is present
Surgery if cancer is suspected
Radiation or chemotherapy if malignant

Complications:
Growth of the nodule
Spread if cancerous
Breathing issues if large or multiple

Prevention and Precautions:
Avoid smoking
Reduce exposure to pollution
Use protective masks in hazardous areas
Regular medical checkups for high-risk individuals
