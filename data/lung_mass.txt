Disease Name: Lung Mass

Overview:
A lung mass is an abnormal growth in the lung that is larger than 3 centimeters in diameter. Masses are usually detected on chest X-rays or CT scans. While some lung masses are benign (non-cancerous), many require careful evaluation because they can be associated with lung cancer or other serious diseases.

A lung mass differs from a lung nodule mainly by size. Nodules are smaller than 3 cm, while masses are larger and have a higher risk of malignancy.

Symptoms:
Some people may have no symptoms. When symptoms occur, they may include:
Persistent cough
Chest pain
Shortness of breath
Coughing up blood
Fatigue
Unexplained weight loss
Frequent lung infections

Causes:
Lung masses may occur due to several conditions such as:
Lung cancer
Benign lung tumors
Infections
Tuberculosis
Fungal infections
Lung abscess
Inflammatory diseases
Scar tissue in the lungs

Risk Factors:
Smoking
Exposure to secondhand smoke
Air pollution
Exposure to asbestos or chemicals
Family history of lung cancer
Chronic lung disease

Diagnosis:
Doctors use several tests to evaluate a lung mass:
Chest X-ray
CT scan
PET scan
Bronchoscopy
Needle biopsy
Sputum cytology

Treatment:
Treatment depends on the cause of the lung mass.

Possible treatments include:
Surgery to remove the mass
Radiation therapy
Chemotherapy
Targeted therapy
Antibiotics or antifungal medicines for infections
Regular monitoring with imaging if the mass appears benign

Complications:
Breathing problems
Spread of cancer to other organs
Lung damage
Infection

Prevention and Precautions:
Avoid smoking
Limit exposure to pollution and toxic chemicals
Use protective equipment in hazardous workplaces
Maintain regular health checkups
Seek medical attention if persistent respiratory symptoms occur
