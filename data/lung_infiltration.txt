Disease Name: Pulmonary Infiltration (Lung Infiltrate)

Overview:
Pulmonary infiltration refers to substances such as fluid, pus, blood, or cells accumulating within lung tissue. These appear as abnormal dense areas on chest imaging such as X-rays or CT scans. It is not a disease itself but a sign of an underlying lung condition.

Symptoms:
Cough
Shortness of breath
Fever
Chest pain
Fatigue
Difficulty breathing

Causes:
Pneumonia
Tuberculosis
Pulmonary edema
Lung hemorrhage
Cancer
Inflammatory diseases
Aspiration of food or liquids
ARDS

Risk Factors:
Smoking
Weak immune system
Chronic lung disease
Hospitalization
Air pollution exposure

Diagnosis:
Chest X-ray
CT scan
Blood tests
Sputum tests
Bronchoscopy
Lung biopsy in severe cases

Treatment:
Antibiotics for bacterial infections
Antiviral or antifungal medications
Anti-inflammatory medicines
Oxygen therapy
Hospital treatment if severe

Complications:
Respiratory failure
Severe infection
Lung damage
Low oxygen levels

Prevention and Precautions:
Avoid smoking
Vaccinations
Good hygiene
Early treatment of infections
Protective masks in polluted areas
