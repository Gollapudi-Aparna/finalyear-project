Disease Name: Atelectasis

Overview:
Atelectasis is the collapse of a lung or a part of a lung (a lobe). It occurs when the tiny air sacs in the lungs, called alveoli, lose air. Atelectasis is one of the most common breathing complications after surgery. It may also occur due to other respiratory conditions such as cystic fibrosis, lung tumors, chest injuries, fluid around the lungs, or weakness of breathing muscles. Inhaling a foreign object can also cause this condition.

Atelectasis can make breathing difficult, especially in people who already have lung disease.

Note:
Atelectasis is different from pneumothorax. Pneumothorax occurs when air leaks into the space between the lungs and the chest wall, which can cause the lung to collapse. Pneumothorax can be one cause of atelectasis.

Symptoms:
- Difficulty breathing
- Rapid and shallow breathing
- Wheezing
- Coughing

When to Seek Medical Help:
Seek immediate medical attention if breathing becomes difficult.

Causes:

1. Obstructive Atelectasis (Blocked Airway)
- Mucus plug
- Foreign body in airway
- Tumor inside the airway

2. Non‑Obstructive Atelectasis (Pressure on the Lung)
- Chest injury
- Pleural effusion
- Pneumonia
- Pneumothorax
- Lung scarring
- Tumor pressing on lung

Risk Factors:
- Difficulty swallowing
- Long bed rest
- Lung diseases such as asthma or cystic fibrosis
- Recent surgery
- General anesthesia
- Weak breathing muscles
- Pain preventing deep breathing
- Smoking

Complications:
- Low blood oxygen (Hypoxemia)
- Pneumonia
- Respiratory failure

Prevention:
Children: Keep small objects away to prevent inhalation.
Adults: Follow breathing exercises and doctor guidance after surgery.
