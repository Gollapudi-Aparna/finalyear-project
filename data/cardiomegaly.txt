Disease Name: Cardiomegaly (Enlarged Heart)

Overview:
Cardiomegaly refers to an enlarged heart. It is usually a sign of another condition that causes the heart to work harder than normal. Doctors measure heart size using imaging tests and determine the underlying cause.

An enlarged heart may be temporary or permanent depending on the cause. The enlargement may involve the entire heart or only certain parts.

Symptoms:
Dizziness
Swelling in legs, feet, or abdomen
Fatigue
Heart palpitations
Low energy
Shortness of breath

Causes:
Coronary artery disease
High blood pressure
Arrhythmia
Cardiomyopathy
Heart valve disease
Congenital heart disease
Anemia
Pregnancy
Thyroid disorders
Kidney failure
Lung diseases such as COPD

Diagnosis:
Chest X-ray
CT scan
Echocardiogram
Electrocardiogram (EKG)
Exercise stress test
Heart MRI
Genetic testing

Treatment:
Treatment focuses on the underlying cause.

Lifestyle changes:
Reduce salt intake
Quit smoking
Limit alcohol
Exercise regularly
Maintain healthy weight

Medications:
ACE inhibitors
Beta blockers
Diuretics
Anticoagulants
Antiarrhythmic drugs

Procedures:
Pacemaker
Implantable cardioverter defibrillator
Valve repair or replacement
Coronary bypass surgery
Stent placement

Complications:
Heart failure
Stroke
Blood clots
Heart attack
Sudden cardiac death

Prevention:
Healthy diet
Regular exercise
Control blood pressure
Maintain healthy weight
Avoid tobacco
Limit alcohol

Emergency Symptoms:
Chest pain
Severe breathing difficulty
Fainting
Pain spreading to arms, neck, jaw or back
